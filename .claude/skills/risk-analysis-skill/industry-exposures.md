## 1. Real Estate Development

### Operational Profile
Developers acquire land, entitle it, finance construction, and either sell or hold/operate
the completed asset. Risk profile shifts dramatically across the development lifecycle:
pre-development (entitlement risk, environmental), construction (builders risk, wrap-up,
contractor default), lease-up/operations (premises liability, property, BI), and disposition.

### Critical Exposures
- **Construction phase**: Builders risk, OCIP/CCIP, contractor default, construction
  defect (especially multifamily residential), delay/soft costs
- **Completed asset**: Property (replacement cost, BI, ordinance or law), GL (premises
  liability, slip/fall, security negligence), environmental (mold, legionella, asbestos
  in renovation)
- **Professional liability**: Development manager E&O, construction management E&O if
  acting as CM
- **D&O / Management liability**: Investor lawsuits over project performance, joint
  venture disputes, fund-level D&O for fund sponsors
- **Environmental**: Phase I/II findings, brownfield redevelopment, PFAS, historical
  contamination, stormwater management
- **Lender requirements**: Replacement cost evidence, named lender endorsements, flood
  compliance, ordinance or law minimums

### Risk Manager Priorities
SOV accuracy and valuation methodology, construction timeline risk, zoning/entitlement
uncertainty, joint venture partner default, general contractor selection and prequalification,
environmental site history, ADA compliance.

### AE Focus Areas
Builders risk structure (project-specific vs. master), OCIP/CCIP feasibility and design,
excess/umbrella adequacy for development-stage liability, D&O for development entities
and fund-level structures, lender compliance coordination.

## 2. Digital Infrastructure / Data Centers

### Operational Profile
Companies that develop, own, and/or operate data centers, colocation facilities, fiber
networks, edge computing sites, and related digital infrastructure. Revenue models include
colocation leasing, powered shell, build-to-suit, hyperscale campuses, and managed services.

### Critical Exposures
- **Property**: Extremely high asset values with concentrated risk; specialized mechanical/
  electrical systems (generators, UPS, cooling); equipment breakdown is paramount; business
  income exposure is massive given tenant SLAs and revenue density per square foot
- **Business interruption**: Revenue per SF far exceeds traditional real estate; SLA
  penalties for downtime; contingent BI from utility/fiber provider failure; interdependency
  with power grid
- **Liability**: Premises liability, tenant property in custody (colocation), errors in
  service delivery, consequential damages from outages
- **Cyber**: While data center operators may not own tenant data, they are custodians of
  availability; DDoS, physical-cyber convergence, OT/ICS systems managing building
  automation
- **Environmental**: Generator fuel storage (diesel, natural gas), battery storage (lithium-ion
  thermal runaway), water usage for cooling, noise ordinance compliance
- **Construction**: Massive capital programs with specialized MEP, long lead-time equipment,
  phased delivery schedules, commissioning risk
- **E&O / Technology E&O**: Service delivery failures, SLA breach, data loss (if managed
  services)
- **Power procurement**: Grid reliability, power purchase agreements, backup generation,
  renewable energy credit exposure

### Risk Manager Priorities
Redundancy architecture (N+1, 2N, 2N+1), utility reliability and dual-feed power, fire
suppression systems (clean agent, pre-action sprinkler), hot aisle/cold aisle containment,
physical security (mantraps, biometrics), generator testing and fuel supply contracts,
commissioning protocols, SLA/penalty exposure quantification.

### AE Focus Areas
Equipment breakdown coverage adequacy (critical for UPS, HVAC, generators), BI waiting
period calibration (every hour of downtime is material), property valuation methodology
for specialized MEP, technology E&O for managed service providers, excess liability tower
height for consequential damage exposure, builders risk for phased development programs,
environmental for fuel storage and battery systems.

## 3. Energy (Renewable & Traditional)

### Operational Profile
Spans solar, wind, battery energy storage (BESS), natural gas, hydrogen, EV charging
infrastructure, transmission/distribution, and hybrid facilities. Risk varies significantly
by technology and project phase.

### Critical Exposures
- **Property / equipment**: Solar panels (hail, theft, degradation), wind turbines
  (blade failure, gearbox, lightning), BESS (thermal runaway, fire, deflagration),
  substations and transformers, inverters and power electronics
- **Business interruption**: Revenue tied to power purchase agreements (PPAs), renewable
  energy credits (RECs), merchant power pricing; long lead-time equipment delays
  restoration significantly
- **Construction**: Large-scale EPC projects with complex commissioning, weather delays,
  interconnection risk, performance testing
- **Liability**: Premises liability (remote sites, public access), wildfire ignition
  (transmission lines, inverter faults), battery storage fire/explosion, pollution from
  fuel storage or transformer PCBs
- **Professional**: Engineering design deficiency, performance modeling errors, interconnection
  study errors, environmental permitting failures
- **Environmental**: Habitat impact, stormwater, decommissioning obligations, hazardous
  materials (transformer oil, battery chemistry)
- **Cyber / OT**: SCADA systems, remote monitoring, grid interconnection points, inverter
  firmware vulnerabilities

### Risk Manager Priorities
Technology risk assessment (proven vs. emerging technology), PPA/offtake contract security,
equipment warranty and manufacturer financial stability, weather/climate data for site
selection, interconnection timeline risk, decommissioning cost estimation, fire prevention
for BESS (spacing, suppression, monitoring), wildlife/environmental compliance.

### AE Focus Areas
Builders risk for renewable construction (DSU/delay in start-up), operational all-risk
property for energy assets, specialized equipment breakdown, parametric weather covers,
BESS-specific property forms, excess liability for wildfire/explosion scenarios, pollution
liability for fuel storage and transformer oil, professional liability for energy
engineering firms.

## 4. Construction (General & Specialty)

### Operational Profile
General contractors, specialty trade contractors, construction managers, design-builders.
Risk profile defined by project type (commercial, residential, infrastructure, industrial),
contract delivery method (DBB, DB, CM-at-risk, IPD), and labor model (union vs. open shop,
self-perform vs. sub-out).

### Critical Exposures
- **GL / Products-completed operations**: Construction defect (especially residential),
  bodily injury on jobsite, damage to existing structures, post-completion liability
- **Workers' compensation**: High-hazard classifications, multi-state operations,
  subcontractor labor, experience modification management
- **Builders risk**: Project-specific or master program, soft costs, testing/commissioning
- **Auto**: Fleet of trucks, heavy equipment, employee vehicles
- **Professional**: CM E&O, design-build E&O, scheduling/cost estimation errors
- **Subcontractor default**: Subcontractor non-performance, financial failure mid-project
- **Pollution**: Soil disturbance, dewatering, demolition (asbestos, lead), fuel storage
- **Surety**: Bid, performance, payment bonds; bonding capacity/program
- **OCIP/CCIP**: Wrap-up programs for large projects — enrollment, audit, close-out

### Risk Manager Priorities
Subcontractor prequalification and insurance verification, contractual risk transfer
(indemnification, additional insured, waiver of subrogation), safety program and EMR
management, project-specific risk assessments, hot work and confined space protocols,
crane/rigging plans, OSHA compliance.

### AE Focus Areas
GL products-completed operations tail, OCIP/CCIP design and feasibility analysis, excess
liability tower for large projects, contractors professional liability, subcontractor
default insurance, builders risk structure, surety program capacity, workers' comp
program design (guaranteed cost vs. loss-sensitive).

## 5. Manufacturing

### Operational Profile
Companies that transform raw materials into finished goods. Risk shaped by product type,
production process, distribution model, supply chain complexity, and regulatory environment.

### Critical Exposures
- **Property**: High-value production equipment, raw materials and finished goods inventory,
  fire and explosion exposure from processes, equipment breakdown
- **Business interruption**: Single-site concentration, specialized equipment with long
  replacement lead times, supply chain disruption (CBI), order cancellation exposure
- **Products liability**: Product failure causing BI/PD, product recall, consumer safety,
  regulatory action
- **Environmental**: Process emissions, wastewater discharge, hazardous material storage,
  historical contamination
- **Workers' comp**: Industrial processes, machinery guarding, repetitive motion, chemical
  exposure
- **Cyber / OT**: Manufacturing execution systems (MES), SCADA/ICS, robotic systems,
  IoT sensors on production lines
- **International**: Global supply chains, foreign operations, import/export compliance

### Risk Manager Priorities
Fire protection and prevention (FM Global / HPR considerations), supply chain mapping and
resilience, product quality systems, OSHA compliance, environmental permits, business
continuity planning, equipment maintenance programs.

### AE Focus Areas
Property valuation methodology (replacement cost of specialized equipment), BI adequacy
with realistic restoration period, equipment breakdown, product recall coverage,
environmental liability (known and unknown conditions), international placements, excess
liability for products exposure.

## 6. Technology / SaaS

### Critical Exposures
Technology E&O (platform failure, SLA breach, data loss), cyber liability (data breach,
ransomware, system outage), D&O (investor litigation, regulatory investigation, fiduciary
duty), IPL (IP infringement defense/abatement), media liability, employment practices
(rapid hiring/firing, classification).

### AE Focus Areas
Tech E&O / cyber package vs. standalone placements, D&O with Side A DIC for venture-backed
companies, IPL coverage availability, excess liability for consequential damages, wage &
hour EPL.

## 7. Healthcare / Life Sciences

### Critical Exposures
Medical professional liability (malpractice), clinical trial liability, product liability
(devices, pharma), cyber/privacy (HIPAA, PHI), regulatory/compliance, D&O, workers' comp
(needlestick, patient handling), property (specialized equipment, temperature-sensitive
inventory).

### AE Focus Areas
Medical professional liability program (occurrence vs. claims-made, tail coverage),
clinical trial liability, product liability for devices/pharma, cyber with HIPAA-specific
response, sexual abuse/molestation coverage, excess liability tower adequacy.

## 8. Transportation & Logistics

### Critical Exposures
Auto liability (large fleet, hired/non-owned), motor carrier liability (MCS-90, BMC-91),
cargo/freight legal liability, workers' comp (high-hazard driving classifications),
warehouse legal liability, pollution (fuel transport, hazmat), cyber (GPS, fleet management
systems, customer data), BI from fleet disruption.

### AE Focus Areas
Fleet auto liability and physical damage, excess auto liability, motor carrier filings,
cargo coverage, pollution liability for hazmat transport, workers' comp for high-hazard
classifications, warehouse legal liability.

## 9. Hospitality & Food Service

### Critical Exposures
GL (slip/fall, food contamination, liquor liability, swimming pool), property (fire,
kitchen grease, flood in coastal locations), workers' comp (kitchen, housekeeping,
repetitive motion), liquor liability, event cancellation, cyber (payment card data,
guest PII), management liability (franchise operations, labor disputes), food contamination /
product recall.

### AE Focus Areas
Property program with adequate BI (seasonal revenue fluctuations, peak period exposure),
liquor liability adequacy, excess liability tower, workers' comp program design, food
contamination / recall coverage, franchise operations coverage (franchisor vs. franchisee),
cyber for PCI compliance.

---

## 10. Financial Services

### Critical Exposures
D&O (securities litigation, regulatory investigation, derivatives disputes), E&O
(investment advice, lending practices, trust administration), cyber (customer financial
data, wire fraud, ATO), crime/fidelity (employee theft, social engineering), fiduciary
liability (retirement plans), EPL (commission-based compensation disputes, restrictive
covenant), bankers professional liability, investment advisor E&O.

### AE Focus Areas
D&O with appropriate Side A, B, C structure, bankers professional / investment advisor E&O,
crime policy with social engineering and cyber crime coverage, cyber liability, fiduciary
liability, excess management liability tower, regulatory investigation coverage.

## Using This Reference

When analyzing a specific organization, identify which industry profile(s) apply — many
organizations span multiple categories (e.g., a real estate developer building data centers
sits across profiles 1, 2, and 4). Use the profiles to ensure completeness in exposure
identification, then cross-reference with `coverage-framework.md` to design the program
response.
